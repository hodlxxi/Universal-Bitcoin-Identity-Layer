# Sybil and Identity

## Purpose
Clarify identity assumptions and Sybil-resistance posture in HODLXXI Trust Domains.

## Identity in HODLXXI
Identity begins with key control, then becomes meaningful through sustained interaction history inside a domain context.

## Why context matters
The same key can appear in multiple domains, but observed reliability may differ by:
- counterparties,
- norms,
- time horizon,
- and Sponsor lineage.

## Sybil-resistance strategy
HODLXXI raises Sybil cost via social-structural filters:
- Sponsor-gated admission paths
- recursive accountability for introduced Branches
- progressive trust conditioned on observable behavior over time

## Non-reduction principle
HODLXXI does not collapse trust into a scalar score. Domain participants evaluate rich history, not a single metric target.

## Operational consequence
Two Trust Domains can reasonably reach different trust conclusions about the same participant. This is expected behavior, not inconsistency.
