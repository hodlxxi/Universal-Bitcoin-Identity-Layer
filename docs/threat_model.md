# Threat Model

## Purpose
Describe the dominant risks in HODLXXI and the local mechanisms used to handle them.

## Main threat categories
- **Sybil pressure**: rapid creation of low-context identities
- **Edge opportunism**: short-term extraction before history accumulates
- **Branch drift**: norm divergence across subgroups over time
- **Meaning shocks**: abrupt reinterpretation of prior commitments

## Mitigation posture
HODLXXI mitigates risk through:
- Sponsor accountability in Trust Trees
- gradual trust expansion based on observed interactions
- Descriptor Pruning (local refusal / local Edge deletion)
- forkability when domains no longer share value assumptions

## Black swan interpretation
For HODLXXI, black swans are usually coordination-level shocks rather than purely financial crashes:
- social meaning changes,
- legitimacy disputes,
- branch-level trust breaks.

Response emphasis is local re-coordination, explicit boundaries, and optional forking.

## Explicit non-goals
- No claim of universal Sybil elimination
- No protocol-level adjudication of social truth
- No guarantee of cross-domain trust compatibility
- No automatic recovery from governance failure
