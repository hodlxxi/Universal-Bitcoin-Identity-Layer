# Covenant Patterns

## Purpose
Explain how covenant patterns fit HODLXXI’s local Trust Domain model without over-claiming what covenants alone can guarantee.

## Framing
In HODLXXI, covenant patterns are long-horizon coordination tools. They can encode durable intent, but trust quality still depends on behavior through time.

## Design role of covenant patterns
- Signal long-term orientation
- Support continuity of obligations
- Create legible commitment anchors for counterparties

## Symbolic long-term lock
A small symbolic stake can be locked on a long horizon (for example, 21-year framing) as a commitment device. The central value remains repeated interaction history, not stake size.

## What covenant patterns contribute
Within a Trust Domain, covenant artifacts can help participants:
- maintain shared expectations,
- document commitment posture,
- coordinate multi-cycle relationships.

## What covenant patterns do not do
- They do not automatically create trust.
- They do not produce universal portability across unrelated domains.
- They do not replace Sponsor accountability or Branch observation.
- They do not mandate one implementation path.
