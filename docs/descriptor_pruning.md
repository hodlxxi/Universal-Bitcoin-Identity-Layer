# Descriptor Pruning

## Purpose
Provide a precise technical and social definition of Descriptor Pruning in HODLXXI.

## Definition
Descriptor Pruning is a local Trust Domain action to stop or constrain cooperation by removing or ignoring local relationship descriptors/Edges.

## Core properties
- local in scope
- policy-driven
- reversible by local choice
- based on observed behavior and risk posture

## Clarifications
Descriptor Pruning is:
- local refusal to interact,
- local edge deletion,
- local boundary maintenance.

Descriptor Pruning is not:
- a protocol-level ban,
- confiscation,
- a Bitcoin consensus rule change,
- a universal global verdict.

## Typical trigger conditions
- repeated Sponsor misalignment
- persistent Branch unreliability
- unresolved policy incompatibility
- unacceptable counterparty risk drift

## Practical example
A family or guild domain can prune a Sponsor Edge after repeated harmful introductions. Other domains may keep or restore that Edge under their own standards.
