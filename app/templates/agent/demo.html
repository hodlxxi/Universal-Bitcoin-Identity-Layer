HODLXXI is a Bitcoin-native proof runtime: pay for an action, get a result, and verify the signed receipt later.

Make one small request. Pay 21 sats. The runtime remembers which public key asked, what was paid, what result was returned, and whether the signed receipt verifies.

Honest identity boundary

The requester key is proven by a compatible Nostr signer before the invoice is created.

This proves control of the signing key for this request. Requester key proof means key control for this request only. It does not prove a legal name, government identity, or that one human controls only one key.

The server binds the signature challenge to the exact message and demo nonce, then includes signature_verified in the signed receipt only after payment.

No private key is requested or exposed; signing happens inside your Nostr extension.

What this proves

I paid, I requested, I received, I can verify.

The proof is limited to bounded recorded facts:

  • a requester public key signed this request;
  • a 21 sat invoice-backed job was created;
  • the runtime recorded the invoice-backed job as settled before issuing the result;
  • the runtime returned a result;
  • the runtime signed a receipt that can be verified later.

What this does not prove

This proof is bounded runtime evidence only: not a token sale; not an investment; not KYC; not legal identity; not custody; not a promise of profit; not proof of moral trustworthiness; not a guarantee of future performance; not ownership of a network.

What the signed receipt remembers

This is the human proof: a public record tying one declared requester key to one paid job, one result, and one agent signature.

Technical endpoint contract

The browser creates the request with POST /agent/request, checks GET /agent/jobs/<job_id>, and verifies with GET /agent/verify/<job_id>.

The payload includes requester_pubkey, message, demo: human_proof_v2, and a unique demo_nonce.
