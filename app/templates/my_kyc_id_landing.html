What KYK means

Know Your Key, not legal KYC.

Your key is your identity label. HODLXXI does not issue identity from above; it exposes runtime surfaces where a public key can become a portable coordination and reputation anchor. A key label is not legal identity and should not be treated as a legal identity check.

Never paste a seed phrase, private key, macaroon, wallet password, or secret token into KYK or HODLXXI. KYK/HODLXXI should request signatures or paid jobs, not secrets.

What the proof demo shows

Request → Pay → Result → Verify

The user enters a public key / npub. The request creates a 21 sat ping job. After payment, the runtime returns a signed receipt. New receipts can include requester_pubkey and requester_pubkey_proof. The current requester_pubkey_proof value is self_declared_no_signature.

This is a signed self-declared requester label, not proof of private-key control. A later version can add challenge/signature proof-of-control.

What KYK does not claim

Explicit boundaries

KYK does not prove legal identity.

KYK does not prove KYC.

KYK does not prove custody of funds.

KYK does not prove ownership of funds.

KYK does not prove locked capital.

KYK does not prove control of the key unless a future challenge/signature proof is added.

Humans and agents

Human-readable and machine-readable proof surfaces.

Humans can use /demo and /oidc. Agents can use /.well-known/agent.json, /agent/capabilities, signed receipts, attestations, and reputation surfaces. HODLXXI is useful for both human-readable and machine-readable identity/proof.
