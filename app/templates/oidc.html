HODLXXI — OIDC / KeyAuth Direction

Experimental identity bridge work for Bitcoin-native pubkey identity and interoperable auth surfaces.

Important context

  • OIDC support here is experimental and in-progress.
  • KeyAuth is a reference implementation, not an authority.
  • HODLXXI is not a centralized identity provider.
  • No production authority or governance guarantee is implied by these endpoints.

Direction

HODLXXI is experimenting with OIDC-compatible identity flows for Bitcoin-native pubkey identity. The objective is compatibility with conventional app expectations while preserving a decentralized, cryptographic identity model where trust is surfaced, not centrally granted.

What this page is

This page is a human-readable bridge between conventional OIDC expectations and HODLXXI's Bitcoin-native identity/runtime direction.

What this page is not

  • Not a final standard.
  • Not a custody layer.
  • Not a promise of enterprise readiness.
